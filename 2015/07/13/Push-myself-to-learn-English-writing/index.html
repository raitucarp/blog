Push myself to learn English writing

2015-07-13 |

Although my English vocabularies are still few, and I don’t know which the best word or phrase to place in each sentence because I confuse the meaning of some words, don’t know the full punctuation works and my grammar is far from perfect, I still push myself to write in English.

As part of my Productivity Journal – it’s written in Indonesian Language (Bahasa Indonesia) as Jurnal Produktivitas, though–, writing an article a day in English is would be such a duty for me in the future.

While I type these words, my brain is still complaining me. And I sometimes use Google Translate as my personal tool, to translate an obvious word from Bahasa Indonesia to English.

And yes, I still don’t know yet how to craft a great content. But with this decision to push myself to write an article in English, I will learn how to craft a great content.

The long term road-map that I wish I could master it is to create a great copy, and great English article content.
Yet, I have to learn from some guide and reading it over and over again until I can master it, as well as I master a computer programming (or learning a new programming language).

Furthermore, as a programmer, I interested in Natural Language Programming. Without a fully understanding of English language, it’s hard to me to create some algorithm based on it.

As nowadays, English is everywhere. I’m going to push myself hard to learn this language as perfect as a native speaker.
Because this is such an introduction post for me to learn English, if you do words count this content, you would find that this article is short (maybe 100-200 words length). That’s fine for me.

I hope this post will open my eyes and my knowledge too, towards how to create a great content and as a reference post from the future.

As a novice this post is far from perfect –full of grammatical error–, I hope you as a reader could understand it.

See you in the next post, the part of productivity journal including learn English roadmap.

The last words. I grateful to do this, and writing this opening roadmap.

Thanks for reading this article so far until this “word”.
